History of Lanzarote

From volcanic origins to a vibrant modern culture, Lanzarote's history is as fascinating as its landscapes.

Early Settlement

The first inhabitants of Lanzarote were the Guanches, an indigenous Berber people who arrived around the first millennium BCE. They lived in caves and simple settlements, leaving behind rock carvings and tools that tell the story of their daily life.

Spanish Conquest

In the early 15th century, Lanzarote was conquered by the Kingdom of Castile under Jean de Béthencourt. The island became a key stopover for explorers, traders, and pirates during the Age of Exploration.

Volcanic Eruptions

Between 1730 and 1736, Lanzarote experienced massive volcanic eruptions, particularly in the Timanfaya region. These eruptions destroyed villages, farmland, and reshaped the island’s unique landscape, creating the stunning lava fields we see today.

Modern Era

In the 20th century, Lanzarote transitioned from an agriculture-based economy to tourism. Artist César Manrique helped shape sustainable development on the island, ensuring that modern resorts blend harmoniously with the natural landscape.

Timeline at a Glance

  • 1st millennium BCE – Guanches inhabit the island
  • 1402 – Spanish conquest by Jean de Béthencourt
  • 1730–1736 – Major volcanic eruptions reshape the island
  • 1970s – César Manrique promotes sustainable tourism and art integration
  • Today – Lanzarote is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve known for tourism, art, and unique landscapes
